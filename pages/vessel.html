Dominant

Dominant: my lean engine for complex interfaces

I wrote Dominant because React's abstractions slowed client work to a crawl. Mutable state, direct DOM bindings, and a single update call keep performance tight and the codebase easy to reason about.

  • - Mutate plain objects and call `d.update()` when you want the DOM to react.
  • - Blend with any DOM API, vanilla JS widget, or legacy surface.
  • - Battle-tested on dashboards, marketing sites, and heavy animation demos.
Webfoundry

Webfoundry: 10x faster sites and apps

Webfoundry is my visual builder layered on Dominant. I iterate with clients in the canvas, wire state through controllers, and push production-ready deploys in days, not months.

  • - Visual Tailwind canvas backed by real JavaScript controllers.
  • - `wf-` bindings keep copy, data, and actions in sync as we iterate.
  • - Netlify-ready builds with code quality that holds up long after launch.
